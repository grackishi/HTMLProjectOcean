About the Abyssal Zone


The abyssal zone is the fourth layer of the ocean, under the bathypelagic zone (midnight zone). It includes depths of around 9,800 to 19,700 feet. It takes up more than 83% of the ocean and covers about 60% of Earth. It is also the largest environment for Earth life, spanning 115,000,000 square miles.

Human activities have little to no affect on the life and ecosystems in the abyssal zone due to it's depth. Abyssal plains are some of the least explored regions on Earth.

There is a low level of energy in the water in the abyssal zone, as it is far from storms on the surface. There is also no sunlight, with average temperatures of 36-37 Â°F and high pressures of 11.000 pounds per inch.

Some of organisms that live in this layer are:

- Glass Sponges

- Deep Sea Jellyfish
- Tripod Fish
- Brittle Stars
- Dumbo Octopuses- have adapted to abyssal environment
- Anglerfish
- Squid
