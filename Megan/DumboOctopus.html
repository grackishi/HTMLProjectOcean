Dumbo Octopus
Scientific name: Grimpoteuthis





About Dumbo Octopuses

The dumbo octopus is the largest cephalopod found below 13,000 feet. It's average size is 8-12 inches, however, the largest ever documented was about 6 feet. It was named after its similarity to the main character of Disney's film Dumbo, because of its prominent "ear" like fins (used with its arms to swim through the water).

Their prey are part of the ecosystem formed by hydrothermal vents mainly consisting of copepods (small crustaceans), bristle worms, and amphipods (also small crustaceans but smaller and have arched backs).
