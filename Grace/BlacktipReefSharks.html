About Blacktip Reef Sharks

Blacktip Reef Sharks are just one of the inhabitants of Coral Reefs. They are smaller sharks that have distinct dark brown or black tips on all of their fins. They can grow to be up to 6 feet long and weigh in around 50 pounds. They spend a lot of their time in shallow waters, including in coral reefs and in mangrove areas. Juvenile sharks are often found in extremely shallow water. The blacktip reef shark is a fast predator and tends to eat reef fish. This includes mullet, herring, surgeonfish, sardines, anchovies, and other small fish.

The blacktip reef shark gernerally avoids human interaction and is frightened by swimmers. This sharks is one of the three most common sharks that inhabits coral reefs in the Indo-Pacific. The other most common sharks includes the Grey Reef Shark and the Whitetip Reef Shark. Some smaller blacktip reef sharks are preyed upon by grey reef sharks, groupers, tiger sharks, and even other blacktip reef sharks.
